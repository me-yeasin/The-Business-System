## 1. Localized Trending Queries (Google Trends BG/RO)
Based on current Eastern European search patterns, merchants are increasingly looking for scalable independence over simple setup.

**Spiking Searches & Trends:**
*   **"Cloudcart миграция към WooCommerce" / "Cloudcart migration"**: A significant trend where merchants scaling past entry-level tiers face a wall of rising platform fees and limited export capabilities (frequent complaints involve "begging support for an export file").
*   **"WooCommerce бавен при много продукти" (WooCommerce slow with many products)**: Merchants migrating away from Cloudcart hit performance walls on WooCommerce when their catalogs exceed 1,000 items.
*   **"Скрити такси SaaS" / "Cloudcart такси"**: General rising discontent with how essential features (like advanced filters or custom courier rules) are gated behind expensive monthly subscriptions.
*   **Courier API Frustrations**: Surges around *"Speedy plugin бави сайта"* (Speedy plugin slows down the site) and *"Econt API грешка при синхронизация"* (Econt API sync error). Romanian expansion searches peak around *"Sameday courier WooCommerce integration Romania."*

## 2. Localized Community Topics (Predpriemach Forum)
From the core Bulgarian e-commerce discussions (Predpriemach forum, LinkedIn BG groups):
*   **Platform Lock-in vs. Open Source Freedom**: There is a hot debate on Cloudcart/SaaS scalability vs. WordPress ownership. The sentiment heavily leans toward WooCommerce, provided the store owner can survive the "spaghetti plugin" phase.
*   **The "Begging for Data" Phenomenon**: Major community complaints revolve around local SaaS platforms intentionally obfuscating product export options, forcing users into difficult scraping methods ("Скрейпване") or manual negotiations to reclaim their own data.
*   **Plugin Bloat & "Spaghetti Code" Anxiety**: Users managing 30+ plugins constantly complain about major WooCommerce updates or courier plugin updates completely breaking checkout and database logic.
*   **Filter Performance Disasters**: General consensus is that native WooCommerce filters and search are unusable for catalogs >1000 products, requiring highly optimized server environments or headless solutions to prevent cart abandonment.
