# Outbound Execution Strategy: The "Risk-Free Proof" System

**Goal:** Secure 5 high-paying Next.js retainer clients (specifically targeting Bulgarian E-commerce / "Ivan the Pragmatist") by leading with speculative value.

---

## Phase 1: Finding & Selecting the Right Prospect
*Refer to `Learnings/lead_qualification_criteria.md` for deep-dive psychology.*

### 1. Where to Look & What to Look For
Do not just scrape random lists. Manually find prospects exhibiting **"Money in Motion"**:
*   **Ad Libraries (Facebook/Google Ads):** Find Bulgarian e-commerce brands actively running ads. Click the ad. If the landing page is slow, ugly, or breaks on mobile, add them to `lead_list.md`.
*   **Google Maps / Reviews:** Look for B2B e-commerce or high-LTV niche suppliers with 100+ positive reviews but an outdated website.

### 2. The Final Qualification Filter
Before sending an email, ensure they pass this test:
*   [ ] **High LTV:** Do they sell products/services where one extra sale justifies our future retainer? (Yes)
*   [ ] **Established:** Do they have proven offline/current revenue? (Yes)
*   [ ] **Technical Gap:** Does their site score poorly on Google PageSpeed Insights (especially mobile)? (Yes)
*   [ ] **No Wantrepreneurs:** Are they a brand-new startup with zero traction? (If Yes, ABORT).

---

## Phase 2: Preparing the Pitch Assets
*Refer to `Learnings/outreach_messaging_lessons.md` for messaging theory.*

Do not build a full Next.js site yet. Choose **ONE** of these low-friction assets to create before you email them:

*   **Option A (The Loom Audit):** Run their site through PageSpeed Insights. Record a 3-minute video showing how their 4-second load time is losing them 15% of buyers, and explain how a headless Next.js architecture solves this.
*   **Option B (The Figma Spec Redesign):** Take a screenshot of their worst-performing (but most important) page—usually the checkout or a high-ticket product page. Do a rapid, beautiful redesign in Figma to show them the "After."

---

## Phase 3: The Outreach Sequence
We do not ask for a 15-minute call. We only ask a simple "Yes or No" question to get them to view the asset.

### Touch 1: The Golden Hook (Day 1)
**Subject:** We redesigned one of your pages. Want to see it? *(or)* Quick question about your mobile checkout

**Body:**
> Hey [First Name],
>
> I was clicking through your ads today and noticed your [product page/checkout] is taking about 4.5 seconds to load on mobile. 
> 
> We actually mocked up a redesigned, Next.js optimized version of that exact page—purely speculative, but we believe it could increase your conversion rate and speed by ~30%.
>
> Should I send the [Figma link / Loom video] over?
>
> – [Your Name]

### Touch 2: The Value Bump (Day 4)
*If they don't reply:*
> Hey [First Name] - didn't want to bury your inbox, but I finished the speed comparison. 
>
> Our Next.js mockup of your checkout is loading in 0.8s compared to the current 4.5s. I recorded a quick 2-minute Loom breaking down exactly why this happens and how to fix it. 
>
> Mind if I drop the link here?

---

## Phase 4: The Handoff & Discovery
Once they say *"Yes, send it,"* or *"Wow, this looks great,"* **THEN** you initiate the sales process.
1. Send the asset.
2. Ask the Ultimate Qualification Question: *"Before we discuss actually building this out for you, who has the final sign-off on technical projects, and what specific conversion goals are you trying to hit this quarter?"*
3. Move them to a Zoom Discovery Call.
