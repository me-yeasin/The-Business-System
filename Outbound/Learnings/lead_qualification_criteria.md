# Lead Qualification Criteria: Refining for Quality

**Context:** Deep-dive research gathered from B2B sales, agency communities (r/agency, r/sales, IndieHackers), and expert case studies. This framework is specifically tailored for our Next.js web development agency's "5 Free Websites" offer to ensure we only target prospects who will become high-value, long-term clients.

---

## 1. Initial Targeting: "Money in Motion" & Tech Gaps
*Before* we even speak to them, the prospect must meet these baseline criteria:
*   **Active Ad Spend + Bad Site:** They are actively spending money on Google/Facebook/LinkedIn Ads but directing traffic to a slow or outdated landing page.
*   **Actively Hiring / Leadership Changes:** They are hiring marketing/sales roles or have a new CMO/VP of Marketing (within 90 days), indicating cash flow and a mandate to improve their digital presence.
*   **High LTV & Offline Success:** Local B2B service providers with 100+ positive Google reviews but a terrible website. Since our upfront offer is a "free website", we need prospects whose Customer Lifetime Value is high enough that they can easily afford backend upsells (hosting, SEO, retainers).
*   **Failing Core Web Vitals:** Using tools like PageSpeed Insights to find businesses failing speed tests, giving us a data-backed pitch for Next.js.

---

## 2. Behavioral "Buying Signals" (Green Flags)
Once we engage them, we look for these nuanced, hard-to-fake traits that separate "decent" prospects from "highly qualified, ready-to-buy" clients:

*   **The "Business Model" Grasp:** A high-intent prospect completely understands their own baseline numbers (LTV, CAC, conversion rate, sales process). They don’t just ask for a "better looking site." They articulate exactly where the leak in their funnel is and ask you to fix it.
*   **Process Compliance:** They respect our sales and onboarding process. If we request a discovery call over Zoom or ask them to fill out a brief, they comply without trying to pull us into their own disjointed operations.
*   **Clear Decision-Making Framework:** High-intent prospects bring the *actual* decision-maker to the discovery call. They can instantly point to a single person or a well-aligned committee who signs off.
*   **Willingness to Appear on Camera:** In modern B2B sales, real intent involves virtual face-to-face engagement. Refusal to use a camera on initial calls correlates strongly with non-committal prospects hiding their lack of authority or budget.
*   **Focus on Scope & ROI over Price:** They evaluate our services as an investment, not an expense. Their questions focus on timelines, scope definitions, and expected return on investment ("How will this increase our pipeline?").

---

## 3. Hard-to-Fake Signals of Quality (During the Call)
*   **Defined Success Criteria:** They can clearly answer "What does success look like for this project?". If they just say "we need more leads" but don't know their close rate, they need a sales process first, not just a website.
*   **Accountability over Blame:** When discussing previous agencies, they explain why they "outgrew" them rather than claiming they were "scammed" or "burned" by three previous vendors.
*   **Realistic Expectations of Complexity:** They don't use phrases like "we just need something simple" to try to anchor a lower price while expecting world-class output. 
*   **No Pre-mature Negotiation:** They do not ask for discounts or negotiate price before they fully understand the scope of work. Early negotiation indicates they value cheapness over results.

---

## 4. Absolute Disqualifiers (Red Flags)
Behaviors that often appear benign but lead to painful engagements:
*   **"Wantrepreneurs":** Never target brand-new startups with zero revenue. They will micromanage the free project, drain time, and lack the budget to convert into paying clients. Target established businesses *only*.
*   **"We'll confirm that later":** Moving fast without having internal decisions made leads to scope creep and infinite revisions.
*   **Overriding Recommendations Early:** If a prospect asks for your expertise but repeatedly overrides your initial recommendations on discovery calls, they will treat you as an "order-taker" rather than a partner.
*   **Ghosting on Deliverables while "Rushing" the Contract:** They push you to sign immediately but vanish when you need assets (logos, copy, access) to actually start work.

---

### 💡 The Ultimate Qualification Question
Before starting any project, ask:
> *"Before we begin, who has the final sign-off on this project, and exactly what pipeline math (traffic -> leads -> close rate) are we trying to improve?"*
