# Cold Outreach Messaging: What Actually Gets Replies

**Context:** Research gathered from r/sales, r/agency, and IndieHackers regarding B2B cold outreach specifically for a web development agency targeting established e-commerce businesses.

---

## 1. The Trap of the "Free Website" Offer
**The Hard Truth:** Pitching a completely "free website" to an established, successful business is actually a **Red Flag**. 
*   **Buyer Perception:** Established owners think, "If it's free, there's a catch" or assume it's going to be a low-quality template. They often fear you will trap them in expensive 1-year hosting/maintenance contracts.
*   **The Pivot:** Instead of giving away a full site, we should offer **"5 Free Performance Mockups"** or a **"Free Speculative Redesign"** of a single high-value page (like a checkout flow). This proves value upfront without making you look cheap or desperate.

---

## 2. What Buyers LOVE (The Winning Template)
Successful agency owners get massive response rates (up to 50%) by offering a risk-free, highly relevant teaser. 

**The Best-Performing Template (From an r/agency case study):**
> **Subject:** We redesigned one of your pages. Want to see it?
> 
> Hey [First Name],
> 
> We mocked up a redesigned version of one of your product pages—purely speculative, but we believe it could increase both conversions and search performance by ~30%.
> 
> Should we send it over?
> 
> – [Your Name]

*Why it works:* It creates curiosity, proves you did your homework, and ends with a "low-friction" ask (they just have to reply "Yes").

---

## 3. The "Loom Video" Framework
Another highly successful strategy is the 3-minute personalized Loom video audit.
*   **The Hook:** Focus strictly on two things the e-commerce owner cares about: **Making more money** and **Brand trust**.
*   **The Message:** *"Mind if I send a short loom video going over how we can improve your checkout conversions?"*
*   **Why it works:** It’s personal and requires zero commitment for the buyer to say yes.

---

## 4. What Buyers HATE (Spammy Mistakes to Avoid)
*   **Talking about the Tech Stack:** Buyers do not care that you write code in Next.js or React. They care about outcomes. 
    * *Instead of saying:* "We build headless Next.js sites."
    * *Say:* "Your current site takes 4.2 seconds to load, losing you an estimated 15% of mobile buyers. We can cut that down to 0.8 seconds."
*   **High-Friction Asks:** Never end your very first email with *"Can we hop on a 15-minute call next Tuesday?"* It asks for too much of their time before you've proven your value.
*   **"Me, Me, Me" Messaging:** Never start emails with *"We are a web development agency based in..."* The email should be 100% about *their* store, *their* slow load times, and *their* lost revenue.
